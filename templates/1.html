Cross sectional study of Prevalence of addiction amongst adolescent and adults in India.

Posted on May 9, 2023 by EMHA

Addiction is a treatable, chronic medical disease involving complex interactions among brain circuits, genetics, the environment, and an individual’s life experiences. People with addiction use substances or engage in behaviours that become compulsive and often continue despite harmful consequences.

Addiction is a complex disease, but nearly a century of scientific study has helped researchers come to a deeper understanding about how it works.

This research has culminated in an important change in how we talk about addiction: Addiction is now classified as a disease that affects the brain, not a personal failing or choice.

Most people think of substance use when they hear about addiction, but that’s not the only type of addiction.

Most experts recognize two types of addiction:

  • Chemical addiction : This refers to addiction that involves the use of substances.
  • Behavioral addiction : This refers to addiction that involves compulsive behaviors. These are persistent, repeated behaviors that you carry out even if they don’t offer any real benefit.

Before getting into the different types of addiction, it’s helpful to understand a few general elements of addiction.

The reward system

Addiction interferes with normal brain function, particularly in the reward system.

When you do something you find enjoyable, whether that’s hanging out with your best friend, drinking a bottle of wine, or using cocaine, this reward system releases the neurotransmitter dopamine along with other chemicals.

Given the stigma associated with substance use and addiction, this is especially true for addiction medicine. Terminology in this area has long presented challenges to clinicians, patients, family members, policy makers, the media, and the general public.

Prevention efforts and treatment approaches for addiction are generally as successful as those for other chronic diseases.

Objectives:

  1. To document the prevalence of any type of addiction amongst adolescent and adults in India

  2. To document the various types of additions amongst the study population

  3. To document their current health status

  4. To find out whether they are in need of any medical/ counselling.

Methodology

Data was collected through Google forms after taking consent and their willingness to participate during 2 to 6 th May 2023.Total 25 participants responded. Participants Identifiers were removed and data was kept confidential and only accessible to investigator for analysis and report formation.

Observations/ Result

Study population comprises of 60.7% males and rest females. Half of the study population was students, rest each 1(4%) were from all walks of occupations like business, consultant, doctor, senior data manger, working in company etc. ( fig 1) More than half (64.3%) study population was 18- 21 years followed by 21-30 years ( 21.3%) and 18-30 years and more than 30 years (each 10.7%). As per reported information most of them (78.6%) don’t have currently no health issue however rest have accepted some health issues. Regarding any type of addiction they had so 81.5 % had some kind of addiction currently only 18. 5 % felt they don’t had any kind of addiction. Most common type of addiction reported was smoking (40%) which is alarming, followed by alcohol, cocaine, black pint, amine. This question was not responded by 5 participants. When specifically asked about alcohol intake 15% accepted they they take alcohol. They take these drugs/ alcohol etc at the time of some happiness to celebrate the event as reported by 38.9% study population followed by before attending any meeting, exam or anytime whenever they feel like and this was responded by 18 participants only. Half of them reported weight gained in last 3 months. When enquired about how do you feel after taking drug 11 Responses were received they include feel happy and sad, drug is love and love is drug, never tried, never seen and not applicable as they perceived them not addicted. Though it’s a sample sample study still 15.4% were in need of any kind of help to get rid of and 34% reported that they are feeling problem in getting sleep (34.6%).

Conclusion : Prevalence of anytype of addiction was 81.5% and specifically alcohol addiction was reported by 18.5% study population. There were 21.4% who had currently some health problem, 34.6% were having difficulty in getting the sleep. There were 15.4% were in need of help in dealing addiction

Limitation:

  1. Small sample size of 25 participant so findings can not be generalized

  2. Non response was high so only 25 participant’s responded,

  3. Study tool : information was collected on Google sheet

  4. Issue was sensitive and stigmatized leading to subject bias.

  5. Google sheet was circulated amongst known circle of friends selection bias are there.

Recommendations

  1. Similar type of study can be done on bigger sample size to reach some conclusion

  2. Interview based study will be more useful in capturing the information

  3. Awareness campaign are required for signs and symptoms of addiction.

  4. Preventive measures like meditation, yoga and spirituality needs to imbibe amongst adolescent.

  5. User friendly counselling centers should be available round the clock to provide counselling and guidance for treatment.

  6. The addiction of any kind shouldn’t be stigmatized, it should be identified as early as possible.

  7. The government can implement drug testing programs in schools and colleges to deter drug use and help identify students who may be at risk of addiction. This can also help schools to identify and intervene early with students who are using drugs.

  8. The government should invest in mental health services to help young people who are struggling with mental health issues that may be contributing to drug addiction. By addressing the root causes of addiction, young people can be empowered to make healthier choices and avoid the dangers of drug addiction.
